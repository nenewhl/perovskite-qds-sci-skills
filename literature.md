# Literature

## Goals

- Build a topic map.
- Identify key methods and benchmark metrics.
- Distinguish established consensus from emerging claims.
- Verify citations, journal instructions, and current policies from official or primary sources.

## Search strategy

Use layered searches:

1. Core material and measurement terms.
2. Mechanism terms.
3. Device/application terms.
4. Benchmark and review terms.
5. Recent-year filters when recency matters.

## Benchmark tables

Include:

- Material composition.
- Processing or synthesis route.
- Key measurement conditions.
- Main performance metrics.
- Controls and stability conditions.
- Source citation.
- Caveats.

## Citation discipline

- Do not fabricate DOIs, journal names, page numbers, or author claims.
- Prefer primary literature for quantitative claims.
- Use reviews for field context, not as the only source for specific metrics.
