# Figures

## Figure strategy

A scientific figure should answer one question. Before designing a figure, define:

- Main claim.
- Required evidence.
- Control or reference.
- Reader path from panel a to final panel.
- Whether the figure belongs in main text, supplementary information, or internal report.

## Style standards

- White background.
- Arial/Helvetica-like fonts.
- Panel letters in consistent position.
- Thin axes and uncluttered legends.
- Colorblind-aware palettes.
- Vector PDF/SVG for plots and schematics whenever possible.
- High-resolution raster exports only for microscopy/images or journal-specific requirements.

## Multi-panel logic

Recommended order:

1. Concept or structure.
2. Synthesis/process or device stack.
3. Core characterization.
4. Mechanistic evidence.
5. Performance benchmark.
6. Stability or reproducibility.

## Quality checks

- Are all axes labeled with units?
- Are controls visible?
- Are sample labels consistent across panels?
- Are fitted curves distinguishable from raw data?
- Are scale bars, integration windows, and normalization methods stated?
- Does the caption explain what is shown, not what the reader should believe?
