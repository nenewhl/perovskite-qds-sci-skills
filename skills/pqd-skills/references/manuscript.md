# PQD Manuscript and Thesis Writing

## Argument Structure

Use a claim-driven structure:

1. Field problem: what blocks PQD performance or understanding?
2. Specific bottleneck: surface defects, phase instability, ligand insulation, ion migration, nonradiative recombination, film formation, device interface, radiation damage, or poor reproducibility.
3. Intervention: synthesis, doping, passivation, ligand exchange, composite embedding, interface design, post-treatment, or device architecture.
4. Evidence chain: structure/composition -> photophysics -> device/function -> stability/mechanism.
5. Boundary: where the method works, where it does not, and what remains unresolved.

## Section Guidance

Title:

- Prefer material + strategy + application/mechanism.
- Avoid inflated claims such as "ultrahigh-performance" unless the metric is central and verified.

Abstract:

- Sentence 1: field problem.
- Sentence 2: missing mechanism or technical bottleneck.
- Sentence 3: what was done.
- Sentence 4: key evidence and quantitative result.
- Sentence 5: implication for PQD science or application.

Introduction:

- Move from broad PQD promise to a narrow unresolved bottleneck.
- Use 3-5 paragraphs: field value, current limitation, prior strategies and gap, this work, contribution.
- Do not overload with generic perovskite history.

Results:

- Start each subsection with the purpose of the experiment, not just the technique.
- Connect panels in causal order. Example: composition control -> defect passivation -> recombination suppression -> device improvement -> stability.
- Put negative or boundary results where they protect credibility.

Discussion:

- Explain mechanism and limits.
- Compare with closest literature using matched metrics.
- Avoid repeating results. Interpret why the data matter.

Conclusion:

- Restate the advance, quantitative outcome, mechanistic insight, and future route.

Thesis:

- Keep broader method detail, additional controls, full datasets, and failed routes.
- Use chapter summaries to connect each dataset to the dissertation's central hypothesis.

## Polishing Rules

- Replace vague intensifiers with quantitative phrases.
- Use "suggests", "indicates", or "is consistent with" when evidence is indirect.
- Use "demonstrates" only when data directly prove the claim.
- Keep mechanism language cautious unless supported by multiple independent measurements.
- For Chinese-to-English drafting, preserve the scientific logic first; then polish sentence rhythm.

## Common PQD Phrases

- "surface-defect-mediated nonradiative recombination"
- "ligand-assisted phase stabilization"
- "halide-vacancy passivation"
- "confined excitonic emission"
- "suppressed ion migration"
- "improved film coupling after ligand exchange"
- "radiation-stable scintillation response"

Use these only when the data justify them.
