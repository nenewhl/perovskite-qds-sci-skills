# PQD Literature Workflow

## Search Strategy

Build searches from three blocks:

- Material block: "perovskite quantum dot", "perovskite nanocrystal", CsPbBr3, CsPbI3, FAPbBr3, lead-free perovskite, doped perovskite nanocrystal.
- Mechanism block: ligand exchange, surface passivation, halide vacancy, phase stability, photoluminescence quantum yield, trap state, ion migration, self-trapped exciton.
- Application block: solar cell, LED, electroluminescence, scintillator, X-ray detector, imaging, lasing, photodetector.

Use broad search first, then filter by application and exact material. For recent claims, search current databases or official publisher pages rather than relying on memory.

## Source Tiers

- Foundational mechanism and methods: Nature family, Science family, Cell Press, Joule, Energy & Environmental Science, Advanced Materials, ACS Energy Letters, Nano Letters, ACS Nano, JACS, Angewandte Chemie, Chemistry of Materials, Advanced Energy Materials, Advanced Functional Materials.
- Device-specific benchmarking: Nature Energy, Nature Photonics, Nature Communications, Joule, Energy & Environmental Science, Advanced Materials, ACS Energy Letters, Advanced Energy Materials.
- Field-wide reviews: use to map topics, then cite primary literature for specific claims.

## Benchmark Tables

For solar cells:

- Material, surface/ligand strategy, film process, device architecture, PCE, Voc, Jsc, FF, stability protocol, area, year, citation.

For LEDs:

- Material/color, passivation/ligand strategy, film/device architecture, EL peak, FWHM, EQE, luminance, lifetime, stability conditions, year, citation.

For X-ray:

- Material/scintillator form, light yield, detection limit, dose range, response time, afterglow, spatial resolution, stability dose, comparator, year, citation.

For photophysics:

- Material, size/composition, PLQY, emission peak/FWHM, lifetime model, passivation mechanism, temperature/atmosphere, year, citation.

## Citation Integrity

- Match each citation to the exact statement it supports.
- Cite original work for specific discoveries or records; cite reviews only for broad background.
- Avoid citation padding. Top journals punish irrelevant or outdated benchmark lists.
- Check whether a cited result is in solution, film, single crystal, glass, composite, or device; do not mix contexts without saying so.

## Novelty Positioning

Frame novelty as one of:

- New material chemistry or phase control.
- New surface/ligand mechanism.
- New photophysical understanding.
- New device performance with convincing statistics.
- New stability under meaningful stress.
- New X-ray/scintillation function or detector architecture.
- New scalable/reproducible processing route.

Avoid novelty claims based only on "first", "highest", or "improved" unless verified by current literature.
