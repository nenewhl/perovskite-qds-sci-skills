# PQD Domain Standards

## Terminology

- Use "perovskite quantum dots" or "perovskite nanocrystals" consistently. Define PQDs at first use.
- Specify composition and dimensionality: e.g. CsPbBr3 QDs, CsPbBr3-xIx, FAPbBr3, Mn-doped CsPbCl3, lead-free Cs2AgBiBr6, quasi-2D, or core/shell.
- Separate "stability" types: storage, thermal, photostability, moisture, oxygen, operational LED stability, solar-cell operational stability, or radiation stability.
- Avoid broad claims such as "excellent stability" without test conditions and comparator.

## Core Characterization

- Structure: XRD phase, peak shifts, crystallite size only when justified, possible secondary phases.
- Morphology: TEM size distribution with n, mean, standard deviation; HRTEM lattice spacing if claiming crystallinity.
- Composition/surface: XPS, EDS, ICP, FTIR, NMR, zeta potential, ligand density, halide ratio, dopant content as relevant.
- Optical: UV-vis absorption, PL, PLQY, FWHM, peak position, Stokes shift, PLE, TRPL, temperature-dependent PL when mechanism requires it.
- Stability: normalized intensity or device metric versus time under clearly stated environment.

## Device Metrics

Solar cells:

- Report Jsc, Voc, FF, PCE, active area, mask area, scan direction, scan rate, hysteresis, stabilized output, EQE-integrated current, batch statistics, and encapsulation/aging conditions.
- For PQD solar cells, describe ligand exchange, film deposition, passivation, interface layers, and thickness if available.

LEDs and emission devices:

- Report EL peak, FWHM, luminance/radiance, EQE, current efficiency, power efficiency, turn-on voltage, CIE coordinates, T50/T95 lifetime, operational current density, and device area.
- Separate PLQY of solution/film from EQE of device.

X-ray scintillation/detection:

- Report light yield, radioluminescence spectrum, detection limit, dose rate, response time, afterglow, spatial resolution, linear dynamic range, stability under dose, and comparison scintillator/detector.
- State irradiation source, tube voltage/current, filtration, distance, dose calibration, and detector readout.

## Common Reviewer Risks

- Unclear halide/composition drift after processing or operation.
- PLQY or lifetime measured in solution while device mechanism is claimed for films.
- Device champion value without batch statistics.
- Stability tested under mild conditions while claimed as practical.
- Benchmarking against outdated or non-comparable records.
- Schematic mechanism unsupported by direct evidence.
