# Domain Standards

## Terminology

Use precise terms for:

- Quantum dots versus nanocrystals.
- Composition, phase, doping/alloying, and surface ligands.
- Optical excitation, emission, linewidth, lifetime, and quantum yield.
- Device-level metrics and measurement geometry.

## Reporting essentials

For materials:

- Composition and synthesis/processing route.
- Purification and storage conditions.
- Ligands or surface treatment when relevant.
- Phase and morphology evidence.
- Optical measurement conditions.

For devices:

- Architecture and active area.
- Measurement atmosphere and calibration.
- Scan direction/rate for photovoltaic curves when relevant.
- EQE, luminance, current density, responsivity, noise, or stability conditions as appropriate.

For imaging/detection:

- Source type or excitation condition.
- Dose, fluence, irradiance, or power density where applicable.
- Geometry, thickness, exposure time, and detector/camera settings.
- Linearity, detection limit, response speed, spatial resolution, and stability where relevant.

## Claim discipline

- Do not call a result stable without time, environment, and retention definition.
- Do not call a device efficient without benchmark context and measurement conditions.
- Do not assign mechanisms based on a single indirect measurement.
