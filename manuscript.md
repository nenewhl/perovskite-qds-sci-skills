# Manuscript

## Writing hierarchy

1. Problem and gap.
2. Material or device strategy.
3. Evidence sequence.
4. Mechanistic interpretation.
5. Benchmarking and limitations.
6. General significance.

## Tone

Use precise academic language. Avoid inflated claims, unsupported novelty statements, and vague descriptors such as excellent, superior, remarkable, or unprecedented unless quantitatively justified.

## Section guidance

- Title: specific, mechanism-aware, and not overclaimed.
- Abstract: include problem, approach, key evidence, benchmark, and implication.
- Introduction: establish gap without excessive review breadth.
- Results: organize by claim, not by instrument order.
- Discussion: separate observation from interpretation.
- Conclusion: summarize contribution and limitations.

## Language checks

- Chemical formulas and units must be consistent.
- Avoid ambiguous pronouns for materials or controls.
- Use past tense for completed experiments and present tense for general knowledge.
